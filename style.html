Apollo 11' was the spacefight that landed the first humans, Americans Neil Armstrong, and Buzz Aldrin, on the Moon on July 20, 1969. at 20:18 UTC. Armstrong became the first to step onto the lunar surface 6 hours later on July 21 at 02-56 UTC
Armstrong spent about three and a half two and a half hoursoutside the spacecraft, Aldrin slightly less; and together they collected 47.5 pounds (21.5 kg) of lunar material for return to Earth. A third member of the mission, Michael Collins, piloted the command spacecraft alone in lunar orbit until Armstrong and Aldrin returned to it for the trip back to Earth
